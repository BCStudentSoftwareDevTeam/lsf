Labor Overload

A labor overload is defined as more than 15 hours of labor per week during regular academic year and may not be approved retroactively. All approvals are subject to periodic review.

Guidlines for Approval:
  • Sophomore, junior, or senior classification
  • Not on any form of probation
  • Enrolled in less than 5 course credits with less than 8 preparations
  • Have a 2.50 GPA, both cumulative and for the previous full term
  • The required 2.50 cumulative GPA may be waived if a 3.00 GPA is earned during the previous full term.

    • Students should NOT work any hours within a secondary assignment until notification of approved Labor Overload. If you believe the student you are completing an overload form for does not meet the requirements above, you may remove them by clicking the red "X" in the table below.

    Labor Requirements

    Labor Requirements: Four-Week May Term
  • Enrolled students must work a minimum of 10 hour per week and may work up to 20 hours per week during time of enrollment.

    • Labor Requirements: Seven-Week Summer Term
  • Students enrolled in more than 1.25 credits must work a minimum of 10 hours per week and may work up to 15 hours a week during time of enrollment.
  • Students enrolled 1.25 credits or less must work a minimum of 10 hours per week and may work up to 32 hours per week during time of enrollment (up to 40 hours per week with permission).
